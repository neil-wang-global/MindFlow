## Original Content

Definition: "Unreliable narration involves the use of an untrustworthy narrator."

Typical form: "Unreliable narrators are usually first-person narrators" — the article also notes a third-person narrator can be unreliable.

Example: Holden Caulfield described as "biased, emotional and juvenile" (J. D. Salinger, The Catcher in the Rye) — used to illustrate deliberate withholding or distortion of information.

Scholarship: the page links to further study of unreliable narration, including research on unreliable third-person narration (see the article titled "Unreliable Third Person Narration? The Case of Katherine Mansfield").
