## Summary
- Aristotle's three modes of persuasion form the foundational framework for persuasive writing: ethos (credibility, established through expertise and fairness), pathos (emotional appeal through vivid language and narrative), and logos (logical appeal through deductive/inductive reasoning and evidence). The enthymeme is "the body of persuasion" -- a rhetorical syllogism engaging the audience by requiring them to supply the missing premise.
