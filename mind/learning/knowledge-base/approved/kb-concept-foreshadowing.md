## Type

## Summary
- Foreshadowing is a narrative device that plants hints about future events to build anticipation, create suspense, and make unlikely events feel credible. It operates through dialogue, plot events, settings, and titles. Related but distinct from Chekhov's gun (which demands necessity) and red herring (which deliberately misleads).
