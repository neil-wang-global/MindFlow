## Original Excerpt
> "Anaphora is the repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses, sentences, or lines. It creates rhythm and emphasis, building rhetorical momentum."

## Candidate Conclusion
- Rhetorical devices are classified into figures of comparison (metaphor, simile, metonymy), figures of repetition (anaphora, epistrophe), and figures of balance (parallelism, antithesis, chiasmus). Anaphora creates rhetorical momentum through beginning-of-clause repetition; parallelism creates balance through grammatical symmetry; antithesis creates emphasis through juxtaposition of opposites in parallel structure.
