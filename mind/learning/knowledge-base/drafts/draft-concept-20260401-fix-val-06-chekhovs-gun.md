## Original Excerpt
> "Chekhov's gun is a narrative principle that \"every element in a story [be] necessary, while irrelevant elements should be removed.\" It is recorded in several letters by Anton Chekhov as advice to young playwrights (Chekhov letters cited, e.g. 1 November 1889)."

## Candidate Conclusion
- Chekhov's gun is a dramatic principle of narrative economy attributed to Anton Chekhov (formalized in his 1889 letter to Lazarev), requiring that every element introduced in a story serve a necessary purpose — if a gun is shown on the wall in Act 1, it must be fired by Act 3.
